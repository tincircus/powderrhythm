- [ ] **1. Form Powder Rhythm LLC.** File Articles of Organization with the OR Secretary of State (~$100), confirm the exact name is available, and get its **EIN** (free, IRS, instant online). The license is issued to the LLC, so it must exist first.

- [ ] **4. Baker City local government recommendation.** Complete Section 1 of the form; submit to the correct Baker City / Baker County office (call to confirm dept — often Planning, City Recorder, or Sheriff). They complete Section 2. Must be done before the OLCC app and references items 1–3.
- [ ] **5. Submit the OLCC application via CAMP.** Limited On-Premises application ($400/yr license fee) online; upload the local government recommendation. ~90-day review begins here.

## Building & fire code — capacity / change of occupancy (separate from OLCC)

_Not an OLCC cost (the $400 is flat), but potentially the bigger workstream. Food is NOT required for the Limited On-Premises license, so no kitchen obligation. Capacity is the real dependency._

- [ ] **Call the Baker City building official / fire marshal** — ask what a change of use from retail store to live-music venue requires at our target capacity.
- [ ] **Set a target occupant load.** Key threshold ~**50 people**: under ~50 can often stay a lighter "Business" occupancy; **50+** pushes into **Assembly (Group A)** classification with heavier requirements.
- [ ] **Change of occupancy review** (if triggered): occupant-load calculation + posting, exits/egress, ADA access, fire evacuation plan, possibly sprinklers depending on size. Build-out cost depends on this — get it scoped early.
- [ ] Note: this ties into the OLCC **local government recommendation** (same city offices, zoning/land-use) and the **insurance quote** (capacity + occupancy type affect the premium).
