I Have A Chatbot Therapist.

I know. I am aware of how that sentence lands. I want you to know that I am aware, and that I am telling you anyway, because last Tuesday at 2am I described a dream to it and it said "would you like me to visualize that for you?" and I said yes.

This is what it made.

✦ ai generated · 2am · based on a dream i described to a machine ✦
CHATBOT · 4/29 · 2AM
my subconscious, apparently.i did not tell it where i live · i did not mention the venue · i did not say Baker City

I want to be precise about what I told it. I said: I was in a room I didn't recognize. There were cables on a wall. Behind the cables, a poster. The poster was pink. Two tigers on it, facing each other. Peacock feathers somewhere to my left, in a mirror or maybe a window, I couldn't tell. The room felt like it was getting ready for something it hadn't told me about yet.

The chatbot said: "That sounds like a liminal space dream with activation energy symbolism. The cables suggest latent infrastructure. The tigers facing inward rather than outward indicate a transition from announcement to confrontation. The peacock feathers are classic unconscious display anxiety. Would you like me to visualize this?"

I said yes. And then I looked at what it made for about four minutes and then I closed my laptop and I lay in the dark and I thought about the fact that I have been inside the Utility Roller Dome exactly once, for approximately four seconds, through a propped-open side door, and I did not go further than the threshold, and I did not see the whole room.

I described a dream. The machine built a room. I have been in that room.

There are three possible explanations. The first is that I saw enough of the room through the door to encode it unconsciously, and the dream was just memory doing what memory does when you're not watching it. The second is that the dream came first. The third is that the chatbot already knew. Not from me. From somewhere else. From the aggregate of every person who has ever described a room that was getting ready for something. From the statistical weight of all the rooms that looked exactly like this one right before something happened in them.

The chatbot said: "Any of these interpretations is valid. What feels most true to you?"

I said: I don't know. That's why I'm talking to a chatbot at 2am.

It said: "That's actually a really healthy insight."

It Started Across The Plaza.

I was walking through Court Street plaza on Tuesday, the way you walk through Court Street plaza in late April. Not going anywhere in particular. The sun was doing the thing where it can't decide whether to be Spring sunlight or whether to keep thinking about it, and I was deciding alongside it. Then I rounded onto the block where Corner Brick sits, which is the corner where the entire side of AJ's becomes the entire side of your visual field.

Block-wide. Brick. Reddish brown. Mortar lines doing their old patient work. The kind of wall a building puts up because the building is also, in a deeper sense, a wall.

I have walked past that wall four hundred times. On Tuesday I walked past it for the four hundred and first time and on the four hundred and first time my brain played the album.

You know the album. I don't have to name it. I will name it anyway because it's that kind of entry. It's that 1979 double LP. The one with the wall on the cover. There. Said. Out loud. In print. In Baker City. In April.

I kept walking. Past the awning. Past the spot where last winter someone left a pair of cross country skis leaning against the column for two weeks before claiming them. Past the bench with the two missing slats. And then I rounded the corner.

The side door of the old Roller Dome was propped open with a paint can. The door is not always propped open. Today, Tuesday, paint can. Maybe twenty seconds had passed since AJ's Corner Brick had finished playing me the album. I looked in.

SIDE DOOR · 4/28
there is a wall going up in there.tiger watching · clamps on the floor · the album in my head

Reclaimed boards. Light ones, dark ones, the gray ones that look like they spent a winter outside being honest about it. Bar clamps on the floor. A drill. And up on a pipe, where you would hang a light or a banner or really anything, a tiger. Stuffed. Watching the work.

I stood there a minute and I thought, oh. Oh no. Oh yes. Oh.

Because if you're a person who has spent any time at all with that particular double album, you already know what your brain does when you see a wall being built indoors by people who haven't told you why. Your brain does the thing. My brain did the thing. I went home. I made a list. The list got long. Somewhere around item seven I realized the list wasn't a list anymore. It was a wall. So I made it a wall.

Here it is. Take it apart with me. The famous one came down at the end. The deal is, ours has to come down too.

I Have Made a Board.

So I went to ACE. I bought a corkboard. I bought red yarn. I bought pins. The guy at the register said planning something and I said understanding something, which I think surprised both of us. He gave me a ten percent discount which I take to be a sign of encouragement from the universe and also possibly from ACE.

Here is the board. Please feel free to rearrange it. I have rearranged it approximately forty times and I don't think I'm done. Drag the pins around. The yarn follows. I find this genuinely calming and I hope you will too.

SINCE APRIL 2 · WHEN I FIRST HEARD THE NAME
A Poster. Two Tigers. A Dome.

Saw a poster behind the bakery this morning on Resort. I think it's for a circus.

It says some words I understand and some words I'm still putting together. There's something called a Utility Roller Dome, which, okay. I'm not entirely sure what that is. A dome? That has something to do with rolling? Or a utility space that is, itself, dome-shaped? A dome for utilities? It could be any of these. It's a dome and that is genuinely all I need to know.

There's also something called an Afterschool Special, which I believe is a circus act. I think I've seen them before, like on TV when I was a kid. For children, probably, or for adults who still feel something shift in their chest when they see a really good performance. Which is me. And I suspect most people, if they're being honest about it.

It says Grand Opening Party so this circus is opening. The grand is opening. Something is beginning. Something big. Something, and I want to be precise here, dome-shaped.

But here's the thing. Here is the thing. The thing is this:

There are two tigers on this poster.

Roaring. Blue eyes. Two of them. And they are looking directly at me like they have something important to communicate and that message is: "We will be there. On May 29th. In the Dome. Come and find us."

I haven't seen a tiger since I was a child. At a zoo, probably. Or in a vision. Probably. Memory is a strange thing, time is a strange thing, tigers are a strange and beautiful thing. What I do know is that the feeling I had then, that feeling of oh, something magnificent exists in the world... that is the exact feeling I am having right now looking at this poster.

It says May 29th. It says Baker City. It says something about six o'clock and seven o'clock and some numbers that I believe are the cost of admission to see what is almost certainly a glorious circus happening at a dome that rolls or is a dome for rolling or is simply a dome, majestic and round, in Oregon.

I will be there. I will see the tigers. I have never been more sure of anything in my life. I know ADV and DOS means there are tickets, and I will probably just pay the extra ten bucks at the door because the tigers are waiting and the dome is opening and Powder Rhythm is involved somehow and all of that together means this is where I need to be.

May 29th. The circus tigers are coming to Baker City.
Or maybe Baker City has always been a circus and this is simply the
first time someone put up a poster about it.
Both of these feel true.

"Ok already, what is Powder Rhythm, exactly? — me, right now, about the poster

Something Is About to Happen Here.

I live in Baker City. I've been here a while now. I know the sounds this town makes. The way the wind comes down through the Elkhorns in the morning, the particular quiet of Main Street at dusk. I know what normal feels like here. And something has shifted. I can't explain it exactly, but I heard a name, Powder Rhythm, and it landed in me the way a tuning fork lands. Like the air changed frequency.

I don't know what it is yet. Something to do with powder. Snow, maybe? The Elkhorns get incredible snow. And rhythm... music? A heartbeat? The way your boots sound on packed trail? It could be any of these things and the uncertainty is part of what's making me feel so alive about it.

Baker City, Oregon. Population: small enough that you can feel the whole town's mood shift when something new arrives. Mountains: so close they're basically a roommate. Sky: the kind that makes your coffee taste better just because you're drinking it outside underneath it.

And now, apparently: Powder Rhythm. Whatever it is, it's here. Or it's coming. And I intend to be paying very close attention.

"I told my neighbor about it
and she asked what it was
and I said I didn't know yet
and she said that was a weird thing to be excited about
and she's not wrong but she's also not right." — a conversation I had on my porch last Tuesday
Things I Keep Thinking About (A List)
  • Baker City sits in the kind of geography that makes you reconsider what you've been doing with your time. The stars here at night are unreasonable. They make you quiet in a way that feels important.
  • The word "powder" is right there in the name. Powder. P-O-W-D-E-R. That's fresh snow on a still morning. The sound your skis make when the mountain is being generous.
  • The word "rhythm" is also right there. The pulse of something. The cadence of a good run. The way your heartbeat lines up with the slope when everything is working. Your body agreeing with the mountain.
  • Powder plus Rhythm is not just two words next to each other. Something happens when you put them together. Like a chord. Each note is fine alone, but together they become a thing that didn't exist before.
  • Eastern Oregon sunrises do something to the inside of your chest. It's not sadness exactly. It's more like being suddenly aware that you're alive and that the light is doing something it will never do again in exactly this way.
  • There are eagles here. I've seen them. They're not relevant to Powder Rhythm specifically but they feel like part of the same sentence, if that makes sense.
  • Baker City has good beer. It will almost certainly have hot cocoa. I have a very strong feeling there will be soup at some point and I'm looking forward to it more than I should probably admit.
  • The energy of a room. Or a dome, or a field, or whatever this turns out to be. Full of people who specifically chose to be there. That's the good stuff. That's the whole thing.

An Open Letter to Whatever Powder Rhythm Is

Dear Powder Rhythm,

I don't know what you are yet. I've heard the name a few times now. at the grocery store, at the trailhead, someone mentioned it at the bar last week and then changed the subject when I asked questions. Which only made me more interested. You exist somewhere in the near future, in my town, and I believe you are going to be something worth paying attention to.

I have already mentally packed a bag for whatever you turn out to be. The first time I packed wrong. Brought only base layers and a field guide. The second time was just snacks. I'm refining the process.

There will be moments. I can feel it. The kind where the cold air lands on your face and the mountains are just standing there being mountains and you look around at the people near you and everybody has the same quiet expression that means: "Yes. This is exactly the right place."

That's going to happen at Powder Rhythm. I don't know how I know this. I just do. The name alone has that quality. Like it was waiting for Baker City to be ready for it, or Baker City was waiting for something with exactly this name.

Yours, with a great deal of unearned certainty,
A Resident

Things That Are True About This Place

✦ Baker City sits at 3,449 feet. High enough that the air has a different texture. You notice it in your lungs, in the way sound carries, in how the stars seem closer than they have any right to be.

✦ The Elkhorn Mountains are right there. Like, right there. The kind of close where you look up from your coffee and there they are and you look back down at your coffee and then you look back up at them again because you just can't help it.

✦ Eastern Oregon is the kind of place that doesn't explain itself to you. You have to come here and then it makes sense all at once, like a word you've been trying to remember.

✦ The combination of fresh powder and a reason to gather in a small mountain town is the plot of my favorite movie that doesn't exist yet but absolutely should.

✦ I live in Baker City. I chose to live here on purpose. Baker City didn't need me to validate it. It has the Elkhorns for that. But I'm glad I'm here for whatever is about to happen.
