# Powder Rhythm Blog — Voice & Style Guide

## What This Site Is

A placeholder for a **record store and music venue** in Baker City, Oregon. Until we're ready to reveal what Powder Rhythm actually is, the site functions as a single-page blog written by a fictional character who lives in Baker City and is trying to piece together what Powder Rhythm is from clues: posters, rumors, overheard conversations, things he's seen around town. He's not in on it. He's just a local guy who is *extremely* stoked about whatever this turns out to be.

## The Character

**Who he is:**
- Lives in Baker City. Loves it there. Has opinions about the Elkhorns.
- A bit of a nerd — not in a tech way, more in a "knows too much about mushroom species and vinyl pressing techniques" way.
- Into outdoor stuff: skiing, hiking, the kind of person who notices what the light is doing at 6:47 AM and has feelings about it.
- Into music. Deeply. The kind of person who will tell you about a B-side unprompted.
- Pretty chill. Drinks tea. Owns fleece. Doesn't rush.
- Has a quiet psychedelic sensibility — not overt, but it shows up in how he describes things. Colors are a little more alive. Patterns feel meaningful. He's comfortable with mystery and doesn't need everything to make sense right away.
- Tongue-in-cheek but also deeply sincere. He knows he's being a little ridiculous. He doesn't care. The excitement is real.

**What he's NOT:**
- Not ironic or detached. He's never performing disinterest.
- Not internet-brained. He doesn't say "slaps," "no notes," "hit different," "manifesting." He talks like a person who reads books and spends time outside.
- Not a conspiracy theorist. He's not paranoid — he's *curious*. He over-interprets normal things because he's excited, not because he's suspicious.
- Not a brand voice. He never sounds like copy. No calls-to-action that feel like marketing.

## The Voice

**Sentence structure:** Meandering but purposeful. He starts somewhere normal and ends up somewhere unexpected. Trailing thoughts, mid-sentence pivots, sentences that break apart and restart. He thinks out loud and keeps discovering new parts of the idea as he talks.

**Punctuation:** Periods do a lot of the work. Short declarative sentences stacked together create his rhythm. Commas for softer joins. Ellipses (...) sparingly, for moments where his thought genuinely trails off into wonder. **Avoid em-dashes (—).** They're too writerly for him. He doesn't construct parenthetical asides; he just says the next thing. The only dashes on the site should be in testimonial attributions (— me, at the gas station).

**Vocabulary:** Warm, specific, slightly elevated. He'll say "magnificent" unironically. He reaches for the precise word but sometimes overshoots in a charming way. Mixes the poetic with the mundane naturally: "the kind of sky that makes your coffee taste better."

**Humor:** Comes from sincerity, not from jokes. The funny parts are funny because he's being completely earnest about something absurd. He doesn't set up punchlines — he just says things that happen to be funny because of how seriously he takes them.

**Emotional register:** Quietly intense. He's not yelling (even when the text is in all caps in the UI — that's the *site's* energy, not his). In the writing itself, he builds. He spirals upward. A paragraph might start with an observation about a poster and end with him thinking about what it felt like to see a tiger as a child.

**Psychedelic undertone:** Shows up as:
- A comfort with not-knowing. "I'm not sure what it is and I don't need to be."
- Sensory richness. He notices textures, light, temperature, the way sound moves.
- A tendency to find connections and patterns — the name "Powder Rhythm" isn't just a name, it's two words that each mean something and together mean something else entirely.
- Occasional moments of genuine awe that feel a little bigger than the situation warrants, but also exactly right.

## The Conceit

He thinks Powder Rhythm might be:
- A circus (the poster has tigers)
- An outdoor festival
- Some kind of art installation
- A secret society for people who love mountains
- All of these at once
- Something he hasn't even imagined yet

He treats every new piece of information like a clue. A poster on a wall. A name overheard at the coffee shop. A flyer stuck under a windshield wiper. Each entry is him processing a new piece of evidence and folding it into his evolving theory.

**He is wrong about almost everything, but his enthusiasm is always right.**

## Tone Traps to Avoid

| Don't | Do Instead |
|---|---|
| "No cap" / "lowkey" / "slaps" / "hits different" | Use his own language. He'd say "there's something about it" or "it does a thing to your chest." |
| "I'm manifesting this" | He'd say "I have a feeling about this" or "something is lining up." |
| "No notes" / "unmatched, undefeated" | He'd describe *why* specifically. He's not a reviewer — he's a person having an experience. |
| Breaking the fourth wall to wink at the audience | Stay in his head. He's writing for himself as much as anyone. |
| Marketing language ("get your tickets!") | He can mention prices and details but as a person relaying info, not selling. |
| Exclamation points everywhere | Use them sparingly. His excitement shows in *what* he says, not punctuation. |
| Em-dashes (—) in prose | Use periods, commas, or ellipses. He doesn't build nested asides. He just says the next thing. |

## Formatting Conventions

- **Entries are dated** and appear newest-first.
- **Navigation** is old-school anchor links. Keep it lo-fi.
- **The UI** (marquees, neon, snowflakes) is the site's personality — loud, psychedelic, alive. The *writing* inside is the character's personality — warm, winding, grounded.
- **Testimonial blocks** are him quoting himself. They should sound like things he actually said out loud to someone who may or may not have been listening.
- **The counter** tracks time since he first heard about Powder Rhythm. It only goes up.

## Example Voice (for reference)

> I found the poster taped to the side of the building on Main Street, the one with the awning that's been leaning since at least 2019. Two tigers. Pink. Spiral background. The kind of design that makes your eyes do a little dance before your brain catches up. It says "Utility Roller Dome" which I think might be a venue but could also be a piece of equipment. It says "Afterschool Special" which I think might be a band but could also be a type of performance involving children or, possibly and more excitingly, tigers. It says May 29th and that's a real date on a real calendar and I've already circled it in pen. Not pencil. Pen.
